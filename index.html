Ronin:

The path to self truth.

Purpose

Centered Heroes

The life of a Ronin, is a wandering path towards one's self truth like the philosophy of Stoicism and finding one's ‘code’ in this world of imposing rule of the Social "Shogun".

In moments like this, to serve the ‘Shogun’ Society while going against one’s anchoring principles, leaves oneself as an empty shell to be whisked away by the heavy currents of their selfish desires. With the world’s ever arching “hold” on society, to go against that rule is to live a life of great shame and dishonor, to face discrimination and to be labeled “Ronin”. However, by following what one believes to be right and moral, one thing is certain, your truth’s and ‘code’ will be tested constantly, and in the moments of wavering weakness, you can turn to the greatest Ronin’s of this world, the great Greek Stoics who chose to anchor one’s self towards principle than self desire, and make changes while facing great tribulation as inspiration. Their beliefs have helped etched their names in the history books of the world!

This Website is a culmination of inspiration for all the Ronin’s in this age that have felt their tether’s towards their self truths may be in peril. Moments of despair and inner conflict, the great stoics of Epictetus, Marcus Aurelius, Seneca, Plato, and many more will help provide perspective by providing “Mantras” or reminders to strengthen one’s resolve and to remember that in every situation, eventually it too shall pass.

Mantras

These quotes or "mantras" are the inspiration for those who are looking for extra needed guidance towards solidifying their minds towards the battle at hand!

"The willing, Destiny guides them. The Unwilling, Destiny drags them."

- Seneca

"Look well into thyself; there is a source of strength which will always spring up if thou wilt always look."

- Marcus Aurelius

"When I see an anxious person, I ask myself, what do they want? For if a person wasn't wanting something outside of their own control, why would they be stricken by anxiety?"

- Epictetus
